Complete Guide To Copper Scrap

A professional handbook for buyers, sellers, recyclers and manufacturers — product categories, ISRI grades, identification and pricing.

Section 1

What Is Copper Scrap?

Copper scrap is copper-containing material no longer needed for its original purpose but recoverable and recyclable. Because copper keeps its electrical conductivity, corrosion resistance and mechanical properties after recycling, it is one of the most valuable recycled metals.

Where it comes from: electrical and power industries, construction and plumbing, automotive, telecoms, industrial machinery, renewable energy and consumer electronics.

Why copper is valuable:

  • Excellent electrical conductivity
  • High thermal conductivity
  • Corrosion resistant
  • Long service life
  • Recyclable repeatedly without quality loss
  • High global demand
Section 2

Copper Product Categories

The physical form or source of the copper scrap.

1. Bare Bright Copper Wire

The highest-value copper scrap: clean, uncoated, unalloyed bright copper wire, free of insulation, solder, paint and oxidation.

Sources: Electrical wiring, cable manufacturing, contractors, rewiring, production offcuts.

Purity / note: 99%+ copper

2. No.1 Copper

Clean unalloyed copper free of excess oxidation, solder, coatings or attachments — does not need to be wire.

Sources: Copper pipe, tubing, busbars, thick sections, sheet copper.

Purity / note: ~99% copper

3. No.2 Copper

Unalloyed copper less clean than No.1 — may have paint, solder, oxidation or light contamination.

Sources: Old plumbing, used pipe, roofing, fabrication, electrical equipment.

Purity / note: Lower than No.1

4. Copper Wire Scrap

Insulated and non-insulated electrical wiring from residential, commercial and industrial sources.

Sources: Bare wire, insulated wire, comms cable, electrical cable, automotive wire.

Purity / note: Insulated is stripped/granulated

5. Copper Cable Scrap

Insulated or armoured cable containing copper conductors.

Sources: Power distribution, telecoms, underground utilities, industrial installations.

Purity / note: Priced on recoverable copper

6. Copper Pipe & Tube

From plumbing, HVAC, refrigeration and industrial installations; usually hollow, may carry fittings/solder.

Sources: Residential plumbing, AC, refrigeration, commercial buildings.

7. Copper Busbar

Solid copper conductors that distribute electricity in switchboards, substations and industrial systems.

Sources: Thick rectangular bars, very high conductivity, high purity.

8. Copper Granules

Small copper pieces produced by mechanically processing wire or cable.

Sources: Used in refining, alloy manufacturing and smelting.

9. Copper Motor Scrap

Copper recovered from electric motors after dismantling — windings, coils, conductors.

Sources: Mixed with steel and aluminium until dismantled.

10. Copper Transformer Scrap

Copper recovered from power and distribution transformers.

Sources: Utilities, industrial plants, renewable energy installations.

11. Copper Radiator Scrap

Copper/brass radiators from older vehicles and industrial equipment; may contain brass, steel brackets and solder.

Sources: Automotive and industrial cooling.

12. Copper Armature Scrap

Copper windings and armatures removed from motors and generators.

Sources: Bought by motor recyclers and smelters.

13. Mixed Copper Scrap

A mixture of copper products not sorted by grade — mixed purity and form, lower value than sorted material.

Sources: All copper sources combined.

Section 3

Common ISRI Copper Grades

ISRI specifications define internationally recognised trading grades. Always verify the latest specification when buying or selling under an ISRI grade name.

Berry

Clean, uncoated, unalloyed copper wire, cable and busbars to ISRI spec — high-purity material for direct recycling.

Candy

No.1 heavy copper: clean unalloyed copper solids such as pipe, tube, plate and busbars with minimal contamination.

Birch / Cliff

Copper wire or cable with insulation requiring further processing before the copper can be recovered (spec depends on insulation type and recovery).

Dream

Light copper scrap — thinner or lightly contaminated copper requiring additional processing.

Ebony

Copper-bearing material with attached non-copper components, requiring separation before refining.

Section 6

Factors Affecting Copper Prices

  • Copper purity
  • Cleanliness
  • Insulation, paint, solder or oil
  • Alloy content
  • Quantity
  • Global copper market prices
  • Local demand
  • Packaging and preparation
Section 7

Worked Examples

Example 1: An electrician removes clean, bright, uncoated copper wiring from a new installation.

Product: Bare Bright Copper Wire. Possible ISRI grade: Berry (if it meets specification).

Example 2: A plumber removes clean copper pipe from a renovation.

Product: Copper Pipe Scrap (No.1 Copper). Possible ISRI grade: Candy (if it meets specification).

Example 3: A contractor removes insulated power cable from an old factory.

Product: Copper Cable Scrap. Possible ISRI grade: Birch or Cliff (depending on cable type and preparation).

Final Summary

When identifying copper scrap, always ask:

1. What is the material? (wire, pipe, cable, motor, radiator)

2. How clean is it? (bare, insulated, oxidised, soldered, mixed)

3. Does it meet an ISRI spec? (Berry, Candy, Birch, etc.)
