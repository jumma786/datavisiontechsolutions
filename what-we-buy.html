Aluminium — All Grades

Aluminium comes in many grades, and each pays differently. Here's every type we buy and exactly what it means.

Aluminium · UBC

UBC — Used Beverage Cans

UBC stands for Used Beverage Cans — everyday aluminium drink cans, typically made from 3004/5182 aluminium alloy. It's one of the most recycled materials in the world, and recycling it uses about 5% of the energy of making new aluminium.

What counts: clean, empty aluminium drink cans, loose or baled.

What lowers the price: liquid still in cans, steel food cans mixed in, plastic bottles, dirt and moisture. Magnet test — aluminium cans are non-magnetic; steel cans stick.

Best price tip: keep cans dry, free of rubbish and steel, and bale them if you have volume.

Aluminium · Extrusion

Extrusion (6063)

Aluminium extrusion is metal pushed through a die into long sections — most commonly 6063 grade window and door frames, curtain walling and architectural profiles. Clean extrusion is one of the highest-paying aluminium grades because it's a consistent, single alloy.

What counts: bare aluminium sections, uncoated or lightly anodised.

What lowers the price: the plastic thermal break, rubber gaskets, glass, steel screws and brush seals. Heavily painted/powder-coated sections drop to a lower grade.

Best price tip: strip out rubber, plastic and steel where you can — clean "EE" extrusion pays the most.

Aluminium · TT

Taint / Tabor (TT)

Taint/Tabor is the trade grade for mixed, low-copper aluminium — soft clippings and sheet that are often painted or coated. Think aluminium siding, painted sheet, mixed soft offcuts and light-gauge aluminium that doesn't qualify as clean sheet or extrusion.

What counts: painted or clean light-gauge aluminium clippings and sheet, low copper/iron content.

What lowers the price: attached steel or iron, heavy paint build-up, insulation foam, and excessive dirt.

Best price tip: separating cleaner sheet out of a Taint/Tabor pile can lift part of it into a higher grade.

Aluminium · Sheet

Aluminium Sheet

Clean rolled aluminium — panels, litho/printing plate, trim and bodywork sheet. Uncoated, single-grade sheet is a strong-paying grade; coated or mixed sheet falls toward Taint/Tabor.

What counts: clean uncoated aluminium sheet and plate, litho plate.

What lowers the price: paint, plastic film, rivets, steel fixings and laminated/composite panels (ACM) which are part-plastic.

Best price tip: keep litho plate separate — it's a clean, premium aluminium sheet grade.

Aluminium · Radiators

Aluminium & Alloy Radiators

Vehicle and air-con radiators come in two main types: all-aluminium radiators and aluminium-copper radiators (mixed metal). They're priced differently because the copper content adds value.

What counts: aluminium radiators, alu/copper radiators, AC condenser coils.

What lowers the price: attached steel brackets, plastic end tanks, oil and refrigerant residue.

Best price tip: keep all-aluminium radiators separate from alu/copper ones — we grade and pay each accordingly.

Aluminium · Cast

Cast Aluminium & Alloy Wheels

Cast aluminium includes engine parts, gearbox casings, brackets, alloy wheels and LM-grade castings, plus machining turnings/swarf. It's a slightly lower grade than wrought aluminium but always in demand.

What counts: cast aluminium parts, alloy wheels (with or without tyres/steel), clean dry turnings.

What lowers the price: steel inserts and bearings, tyres still on wheels, oil-soaked or wet turnings.

Best price tip: alloy wheels with the tyre and steel weights removed pay more; keep turnings dry and oil-free.

Non-Ferrous & More

Every Other Metal We Buy

Top Value

Copper

Copper is our highest-value everyday metal and is graded by purity:

  • Bright/bare bright (No.1): clean, unalloyed bare copper wire — the top grade.
  • No.2 / braziery: copper with solder, paint or light tarnish.
  • Copper tube/pipe: clean plumbing tube, free of fittings and solder.
  • Heavy copper: bus-bar and thick clean copper.

Best price tip: stripping cable to bare bright copper lifts it well above insulated cable rates.

Non-Ferrous

Brass

Brass covers fittings, valves, taps, locks, ornaments, gunmetal and brass turnings. We sort and grade mixed brass and pay on quality.

What lowers the price: attached steel, heavy plating, and mixed-in zinc/pot metal.

Best price tip: separate clean yellow brass from plumbing/gunmetal where possible.
