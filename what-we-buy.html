Pricing Guide

What actually sets your price

Scrap prices can seem to move for no reason — but every figure at the weighbridge traces back to a few clear factors:

  • The LME benchmark. Most non-ferrous metals are priced against the London Metal Exchange. Your scrap isn't pure refined metal, so it's discounted from that benchmark — but the LME is where it all starts.
  • Grade & cleanliness. Clean, single-grade material is worth far more than mixed or contaminated loads. Bright copper wire beats braziery copper; clean aluminium beats painted or attached scrap.
  • Form. Sorted, baled material is worth more than loose, dirty loads that still need processing.
  • Volume & logistics. Larger, regular loads that are efficient to collect attract better rates.
  • Market timing. Metal markets soften and strengthen through the year, tracking energy costs and export demand.

FAQs

What we buy — common questions

What scrap metals do you buy?
We buy all grades of ferrous and non-ferrous metal including copper, aluminium, brass, stainless steel, lead and cable, plus ferrous (steel and iron), end-of-life vehicles, plant and machinery, and mixed factory scrap.

How is scrap metal priced?
Prices start from the London Metal Exchange (LME) benchmark for refined metal, then are adjusted for grade, cleanliness, contamination, form and volume. Cleaner, well-sorted material is paid closest to the benchmark.

How can I get a better price for my scrap?
Separate metals by grade at source, keep them clean and free of attached steel, plastic or moisture, and present larger, regular loads. Sorting is the single biggest factor within your control.

Do you buy mixed or unsorted loads?
Yes. We buy both sorted and mixed loads. Mixed material that still needs processing is paid at a lower rate than clean, sorted grades, but we're happy to quote on any load.
