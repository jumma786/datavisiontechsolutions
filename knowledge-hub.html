What are the different grades of aluminium scrap?

Aluminium scrap is graded by alloy family and cleanliness — broadly clean wrought (extrusion, sheet), cast, used beverage cans (UBC), and mixed or coated material, each priced differently. The trade uses ISRI codes (e.g. Tabloid, Taldon, Twitch) as a common language.

What is 'tabloid' aluminium scrap?

'Tabloid' is an ISRI code for clean, low-copper aluminium scrap — typically clean sheet or extrusion free of attachments. It is a high-value wrought grade because its chemistry remelts predictably.

What does ISRI grade mean for aluminium scrap?

ISRI codes are the scrap industry's standard names (e.g. Tabloid, Taldon, Tense, Twitch) defining each grade's composition and cleanliness. They give buyers and sellers a common language so a load is priced consistently.

How do I tell cast aluminium from wrought aluminium?

Cast aluminium is moulded into shapes (engine parts, brackets) and tends to snap rather than bend, while wrought aluminium is rolled or extruded (sheet, frames, profiles) and bends. They are different alloy families and are priced and kept separate.

What is the difference between 6xxx and 3xxx aluminium scrap?

6xxx is the extrusion family (magnesium-silicon — window frames, profiles); 3xxx is a manganese sheet family (panels, some cans). They have different chemistries and value, so dealers keep them separate to preserve grade.

What counts as clean aluminium extrusion scrap?

Clean extrusion is aluminium profile (typically 6xxx alloy) free of thermal-break plastic, screws, rubber and other attachments. Because its chemistry is predictable and it remelts cleanly, it is one of the highest-value grades and our priority stream.

What is twitch aluminium scrap?

'Twitch' is the ISRI name for floated aluminium from shredded scrap — small clean aluminium pieces recovered by sink-float separation. It is a recognised traded grade used in remelting.

Why do dealers separate aluminium scrap by alloy?

Because each alloy family has a different chemistry and end use; mixing them lowers the value of the whole load. Keeping extrusion, cast, sheet and cans separate means each is paid at its true grade price.

Aluminium scrap prices

How much is aluminium scrap worth per kg in the UK?

Aluminium scrap typically trades at a fraction of the LME aluminium price, varying by grade — clean extrusion and UBC fetch more than mixed or painted material. Prices move daily with the market, so ask for a current quote on your specific grade.

What is the current aluminium scrap price in Birmingham?

Aluminium scrap prices move daily with the LME and vary by grade, so we do not publish a fixed figure. Contact us for a current quote on your specific grade — clean extrusion and UBC price highest.

How is aluminium scrap price calculated?

It is benchmarked against the London Metal Exchange (LME) aluminium price, then discounted by grade, contamination, processing and the dealer's margin. That is why a clean known grade earns far more than a mixed load.

Why is clean aluminium scrap worth more than mixed?

Clean, single-grade aluminium has known chemistry and remelts with minimal loss, so it earns close to its grade value. Mixed or contaminated material must be sorted or carries melt loss, so it is discounted.

What's the price difference between cast and wrought aluminium scrap?

Wrought grades (clean extrusion, sheet) generally price above cast because their chemistry is more predictable and they remelt with less loss. Exact spreads move with the market — ask for a current quote.

How much do aluminium cans sell for as scrap in the UK?

Used beverage cans (UBC) trade as a distinct grade priced off the aluminium market; value depends on whether they are loose, baled, clean and dry. Ask for today's UBC rate on your quantity.

What affects the price of aluminium extrusion scrap?

Four things: the alloy grade, how clean it is (attachments and contamination are deducted), any coating or paint (burn-off loss), and moisture or dirt. Clean, single-grade, dry loads always price best.

Is aluminium scrap price linked to the LME?

Yes. The London Metal Exchange (LME) sets the world benchmark price for primary aluminium; scrap is priced at a discount to it by grade, cleanliness and processing cost. When the LME moves, scrap prices follow.

Law, licensing and payment

How do scrap metal dealers pay for aluminium?

By bank transfer or cheque only — paying cash for scrap metal has been illegal in England and Wales since 2013. We pay every supplier electronically against an invoice and weighbridge ticket, which also gives you clean records for your own accounts.

Why can't scrap dealers pay cash in the UK?

Cash payment for scrap metal was banned by the Scrap Metal Dealers Act 2013 to make transactions traceable and cut metal theft. Payment must be by bank transfer or cheque — we pay strictly by bank transfer.

What paperwork do I need to sell scrap metal in the UK?

You will need valid photo ID, and for trade sellers a waste transfer note documenting the movement of the material. We handle the transfer note on every collection and issue a record of payment, so your duty-of-care paperwork is covered.

Do I need a licence to deal in scrap metal in the UK?

Yes — anyone trading in scrap metal must hold a scrap metal dealer licence from their local council under the Scrap Metal Dealers Act 2013. Data Vision operates fully within the Act, with traceable payments and waste carrier registration.

What is a scrap metal dealer licence and who issues it?

It is a licence required under the Scrap Metal Dealers Act 2013, issued by the local council (in our case Birmingham City Council). It comes as a site licence or a collector's licence, and every holder appears on a public register.

Is it legal to pay cash for scrap metal in the UK?

No. The Scrap Metal Dealers Act 2013 bans cash payment for scrap metal in England and Wales; all payment must be traceable. Any dealer offering cash is trading illegally — we pay strictly by bank transfer.

What is a registered waste carrier?

A registered waste carrier is a business authorised by the Environment Agency to legally transport waste, including scrap metal. Using a registered carrier protects you under duty-of-care rules — we are EA registered (CBDU622654).

How do I check if a scrap dealer is licensed in Birmingham?

Every licensed dealer appears on the national scrap metal dealers public register, and Birmingham City Council holds the local record. Ask any dealer for their licence number and check it before you sell.

What's the difference between a site and a collector's licence?

A site licence lets a dealer buy and store scrap at a fixed premises; a collector's licence only permits travelling collection within a council area and bars taking metal back to a site to sell. The type a dealer holds tells you how they are allowed to operate.

Export and global supply

How is aluminium scrap exported from the UK?

Graded aluminium scrap is baled or loose-loaded into shipping containers and exported under green-list (Annex VII) waste-shipment rules with full customs paperwork. We manage the documentation and logistics end to end.

Which countries import UK aluminium scrap?

The UK exports aluminium scrap to several manufacturing economies, with India among the largest destinations. Data Vision ships graded scrap to our partner works, RA Recycle Service in Rajasthan, India, where it is remelted into product.

How is aluminium scrap shipped from the UK to India?

We consolidate graded scrap, load it into containers at UK ports, and ship it to our partner works RA Recycle Service in Rajasthan, India, with full Annex VII and customs documentation managed at both ends.

What documents are needed to export scrap metal from the UK?

Green-list metal scrap moves under Annex VII waste-shipment documentation, plus standard export paperwork (EORI, commercial invoice) and, for some destinations, a pre-shipment inspection certificate (PSIC). We handle the documentation on every container.

What is a PSIC certificate for scrap export?

A Pre-Shipment Inspection Certificate (PSIC) is an inspection document some importing countries require to confirm a scrap consignment is as declared and free of prohibited material. We arrange it where the destination requires it.

What HS code is used for aluminium scrap?

Aluminium waste and scrap is classified under HS heading 7602. The correct code matters for customs clearance on both the export and import side.

How does a closed-loop aluminium recycling supply chain work?

Scrap is collected, graded and processed, then remelted into new aluminium product that re-enters manufacturing without re-mining. Data Vision sources and grades in the UK; RA Recycle Service remelts in India, closing the loop.
